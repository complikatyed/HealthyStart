First Trimester

months 1-3

  • No menstrual cycle
  • Upset stomach, perhaps with vomiting
  • Increased urination, gassiness, and constipation
  • Breasts may be bigger and/or more tender
  • Headaches
    • Call your doctor before taking medicine!
  • Increased appetite
  • Increased moodiness
  • Increased tiredness

  • Get regular medical care (a visit each month).
  • Call your doctor before taking any medicines.
  • Continue taking prenatal vitamins.
  • Folate-rich foods help spinal cord development.
    • spinach, kale, collard greens, broccoli,
    • kidney beans, lentils, asparagus, avocado,
    • oranges, tomato juice, sunflower seeds
  • You need lots of fluids (water is best).
  • Avoid second-hand smoke as much as you can.
    • Get help to stop smoking.
    • Don't hang out where people are smoking.
Get medical help immediately if you have:
  • bleeding or cramping (like a period)
  • continuous vomiting

  • Carry small snacks to avoid nausea.
  • Pregnancy can affect your memory.
    • Start using a pocket calendar or planner.
    • Try the calendar app on your phone.
  • Your school nurse can help with things like:
    • P.E. - unless your doctor or midwife says otherwise, some exercise is better than none.
    • Standard School Attire adaptations.
    • Referrals for additional support.
