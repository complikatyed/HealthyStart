Life With a Newborn

exciting and exhausting

  • Be sure to ask questions about self-care while you're still at the hospital.
  • Continue taking prenatal vitamins if you're breastfeeding.
  • Make an appointment now for a six-week check up with your doctor.
  • Start using a family planning method.
  • You'll be tired, so try to catch up on your own sleep while the baby naps.
  • Accept help from friends and relatives so you can nap, grab a shower, etc.
  • Get help if you're experiencing symptoms of postpartum depression:
    • Frequent crying
    • Bursts of anger
    • Lack of interest in your baby, your friends, or usual activities
    • Little or no appetite
    • Feeling no emotions
  • Postpartum support and counseling:
    • Hope Clinic for Women
    • Tied Together of Martha O'Bryan

  • Breastfeed your baby if you can.
  • Make sure your baby sleeps in his or her own crib, bassinette, or pack-n-play.
  • See your baby's pediatrican for a 2 week follow-up appointment.
  • Reasons to call your baby's doctor:
    • poor feeding
    • yellowing of the skin or eyes
    • excessive crying
    • forceful or frequent vomiting
    • high temperature (over 100.2°)
    • low temperature (under 98.0°)
    • difficulty breathing
    • choking when feeding

  • Make sure your school knows that your baby has arrived so you can start using Metro's homebound services.
  • When you're ready to go back to school, remember that childcare vouchers are available from the state.
    • Childcare Assistance Resources
  • Alternative schools with more flexible schedules:
    • The Academy at Opry Mills
    • The Academy at Old Cockrill
    • The Academy at Hickory Hollow

  • Having a new baby can be overwhelming.
  • Make sure you're taking advantage of the many supprt programs that are available in Nashville.
  • Home Visitation Resources
  • Parenting Classes
  • Parents Know, Kids Grow
  • Kid Central Tennessee
  • Free Children's Books
  • Counseling and Support Groups
    • Tennessee Voices for Children
    • Tied Together
    • Hope Clinic's BRIDGE Program

  • Apply for baby's birth certificate and social security card.
    • You can often take care of these first two steps while in the hospital:
    • Birth Certificate Resources
    • Social Security Card Resources
  • Take care of insurance, either by adding the baby to your current plan, or by applying for TennCare
  • Apply for childcare vouchers from the state of Tennessee:
    • Childcare Assistance Resources
