Once Your Baby Is Born

life with a newborn

  • Ask questions about self-care while you're still at the hospital.
  • Continue taking prenatal vitamins if you're breastfeeding.
  • Make an appointment for a 6-week check up with your doctor.
  • Start using a family planning method.
  • Get help if you're experiencing symptoms of postpartum depression::
    • Frequent crying
    • Bursts of anger
    • Lack of interest in baby, friends, activities
    • Little or no appetite
    • Feeling no emotions
  • Postpartum support and counseling:
    • Hope Clinic for Women
    • POST PARTUM DEPRESSION SUPPORT
    • POST PARTUM DEPRESSION SUPPORT
    • POST PARTUM DEPRESSION SUPPORT
  • You'll be tired, so try to nap when the baby does
  • Accept help when people offer to keep the baby so you can nap, grab a shower, etc.
  • Breastfeed your baby if you can.
  • Make sure your baby sleeps in his or her own crib, bassinette, or pack-n-play.
  • See your pediatrican for a 2 week follow-up appointment.
  • Reasons to call your baby's doctor:
    • poor feeding
    • yellowing of the skin or eyes
    • excessive crying
    • forceful or frequent vomiting
    • high temperature (over 100.2)
    • low temperature (under 98.0)
    • difficulty breathing
    • choking when feeding
  • Make sure your school counselor knows you've had the baby.

    • because your teachers will want to know
