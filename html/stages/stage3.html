Third Trimester

months 7 to 9

  • Most women find the third trimester physically uncomfortable. You'll probably experience several of the following:
  • Discomfort as the baby stretches & kicks
  • Bleeding gums
  • Swelling of the ankles and feet
  • Decreased ability to sleep well
  • Increased clumsiness
  • Leg cramps, aches in your back or pelvis
  • Practice contractions
    • (called "Braxton Hicks" contractions)

  • Your prenatal appointment schedule will increase to a visit every 2 weeks, then to once a week in your ninth month.
  • Your doctor may order tests to check for gestational diabetes.
  • Start tracking your baby's kicks. You should feel at least 10 kicks per hour.
  • Know the signs of preterm labor:
    • A backache that doesn't go away
    • Contractions every 10 minutes or less
    • Cramping, like gas or period pains
    • Fluid leaking from your vagina
    • Flu-like symptoms - nausea, vomiting
    • Vaginal bleeding - even light bleeding
    • Increased vaginal or pelvic pressure
    • Increased vaginal discharge

    Get medical help immediately for:
    • vaginal bleeding or cramping
      • (like a period, or worse)
    • chest pain or difficulty breathing,
    • fainting or blurred vision
    • severe headache
    • fewer than 10 baby kicks in an hour
    • if your water breaks

  • Teachers will understand that you may have difficulty sitting for an entire class.
  • Right after your baby is born, you can use Metro's homebound studies program.
    • There are forms you'll need to download and fill out in advance:
    • MNPS Homebound Program
  • Talk with your school counselor about returning to school after the baby is born.
  • If you plan to breastfeed, you'll need:
    • a place at school to pump your milk
    • refrigerated storage for the milk
  • Metro also offers several alternative schools with flexible schedules:
    • The Academy at Opry Mills
    • The Academy at Old Cockrill
    • The Academy at Hickory Hollow

  • Talk with your doctor or midwife about:
    • available birthing classes
    • creating a birth plan
    • family planning options
      • (for after the baby is born)
  • Start making arrangements for childcare:
    • The state of Tennessee offers vouchers that can help you pay for childcare.
      You can apply once the baby is born:
    • Childcare Assistance Resources
  • Identify a pediatrician for the baby
  • Pack your bag for the hospital.
  • You'll need a rear-facing infant carseat.
  • For safe sleeping, your baby needs:
    • a crib, or
    • a bassinette, or
    • a "pack and play"
