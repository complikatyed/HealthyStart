First Trimester

months 1-3

  • No menstrual cycle
  • Upset stomach, perhaps with vomiting
  • Increased urination, gassiness, or constipation
  • Breasts may be bigger and/or more tender
  • Headaches
    • Ask your doctor before taking medicine!
  • Increased appetite
  • Increased moodiness
  • Increased tiredness

  • Get regular medical care.
    • (at least one visit every month)
  • Ask your doctor before taking medicine.
  • Continue taking prenatal vitamins.
  • Eat lots of folate-rich foods like:
    • spinach, kale, collard greens, broccoli, kidney and pinto beans, lentils, avocado, oranges, tomato juice, asparagus, okra, and sunflower seeds
    • (They help spinal cord development)
  • Try to avoid second-hand smoke:
    • Get help to stop smoking.
    • Don't hang out while others smoke.
  • Drink lots of fluids (water is best).
    • Get medical help immediately if you have:
    • bleeding or cramping (like a period)
    • continuous vomiting

  • Carry small snacks to avoid nausea.
  • Pregnancy can affect your memory.
    • Use a pocket calendar or planner.
    • Try the calendar app on your phone.
  • Your school nurse can help with:
    • Modified P.E. Options
    • Standard School Attire adaptations.
    • Referrals for additional support.

  • See if you qualify for food assistance:
    • WIC (Women, Infants and Children)
    • 615-340-5368
    • Manna, food support program
    • 615-385-2286
  • Home visiting programs
