Second Trimester

months 4 to 6

  • Your pregnancy will probably be visible by now.
  • Most women feel less nausea and/or vomiting.
  • Bloating and constipation may continue.
  • Headaches or stuffy nose and ears are common.
  • You may feel more tired this trimester.
  • Some faintness or dizziness are typical.
  • Increasing heartburn accompanies more hunger.
  • You'll probably have swollen ankles and feet
  • Back pain is a common complaint.
  • As your belly grows, it may feel itchy.
    • Shea or cocoa butter moisturizers can help.
  • Best of all, you'll start feeling your baby move.

  • Keep attending monthly prenatal appointments.
  • You'll probably have your first ultrasound.
    • The procedure is painless, though the conducting gel is often cold.
    • You'll be able to hear your baby's heartbeat.
    • You will often be given a picture of your baby.
    • It may be possible see if your baby is a boy or girl.
  • Additional tests may be recommended:
    • to check for any genetic problems,
    • to confirm that baby is growing and gaining weight,
    • to make sure you are staying well, too.
  • As the baby presses on your diaphragm, you may feel breathless.
  • Get medical help immediately if you have:
    • bleeding or cramping (like a period, or worse)
    • chest pain or difficulty breathing
    • fainting, blurred vision, or severe headache

  • Keep using your planner or calendar app.
  • You may need to put your feet up during class.
  • Going up and down stairs may be harder.
  • Several small meals are better than one big one.
  • Avoid carrying a load of heavy books.
    • Ask to borrow a copy of textbooks (leave one at home, the other at school).
    • If you must carry lots of books, swap your backpack or bookbag for two smaller totes.
  • Get support from counselors, nurses, or teachers.

  • If you're using WIC, recertify as needed to continue getting aid.
  • Sign up for a home visitation program, if you haven't already done so.
