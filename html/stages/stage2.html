Second Trimester

months 4 to 6

  • Your pregnancy may be visible by now.
  • Less nausea and/or vomiting.
  • Bloating and constipation may continue.
  • Headaches and stuffy nose or ears.
  • You may feel more tired this trimester.
  • Some faintness or dizziness are typical.
  • Increasing hunger and heartburn.
  • You may have swollen ankles and feet
  • Back pain is a common complaint.
  • As your belly grows, it may feel itchy.
    • Try shea or cocoa butter moisturizers.
  • You'll start feeling your baby move.

  • Keep going to your monthly prenatal medical appointments.
  • At least one appointment will be for an ultrasound:
    • The procedure is painless, but the conducting gel is often cold.
    • You'll hear your baby's heartbeat.
    • You may get a picture of your baby.
    • You might even get to find out whether you're having a boy or girl.
  • Additional tests may be recommended:
    • to check for any genetic problems,
    • to see how your baby is growing,
    • to make sure you're staying well
  • As the baby grows and presses on your diaphragm, you may feel breathless.
    • Get medical help immediately if you have:
    • bleeding or cramping
    • chest pain or difficulty breathing
    • fainting, blurred vision, or a severe headache

  • Keep using your planner or calendar app.
  • Ask to put your feet up during class.
  • Going up and down stairs may be harder.
  • Several small meals may digest more easily than one big one.
  • Avoid carrying a load of heavy books.
    • Ask to borrow a copy of textbooks (leave one at home, one at school).
    • If you must carry lots of books, two small totes are better than one large bookbag or backpack.
  • Accept support from your teachers and school counselors or nurses.

  • If you're using WIC, recertify as needed to continue getting aid.
  • Sign up for a home visitation program, if you haven't already done so.
